What I do!

Mario Martin's love for storytelling originated as a young boy when he felt inspired to tell stories through writing. At eighteen he shot his first film, “Checkmate.” That was the birthing of his passion for film-making and screenwriting.

Early on he honed his craft at the Maine Media Workshops and Boston Film & Video Foundation. Mario has attended many screenwriting boot-camps, worked with multiple coverage companies and many screenwriters.

Services

Services provided by phone and email.

  • Action lines. This is where the story is shown. It can only be what the audience see's happening, the actions that are king place. Not what we think and feel.
  • Four throughlines. It's mission critical to follow the four throughlines for effective clear impactful storytelling.
  • Character development. Create memorable characters through the use of characterization.
  • Scene development. Create powerful memorable scenes that engage.
  • Dialogue. Less is more, to much dialogue kills. What are you trying to say? Does the dialogue fit the character and the ene?
  • Plot. Know your plot. There are only seven. Which is yours? Start your story with the plot in mind.
  • Genre. Only seven story premises but many genre's. What's your genre? Is your style of writing reflective of your choice of genre?
  • Pacing. Learn how to tell your story as quickly efficiently and effectively as possible.
  • Structure. Move from the first second and third act seamlessly.
  • Tone. Give your story its own feel and sound.
  • Logline. Write and pitch your feature film in three sentences or less.
  • Synopsis. Write and tell your entire story in four hundred and fifty words or less
